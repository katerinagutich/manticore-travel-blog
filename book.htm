Q&A

What will I learn from this book?

This book will give you the confidence to travel anywhere and be sure that you'll have an amazing time! Whether it's planning a backpacking trip, gap year or career break, a round-the-world trip, or simply a shorter trip to parts unknown, by the time you turn the final page you'll know that you've got this.

We'll unpack the many challenges and uncertainties that all travelers face, then reveal the secrets to finding adventure no matter how or where you travel. Above all, this in-depth guide will lay to rest all the little worries you may have before a trip — and turn it into pure excitement!

Okay, but what will I REALLY learn?

Besides inspiring you to travel, I will also help you with every practical step of planning your trip, from deciding you want to travel to the moment you step onto that plane.

You’ll learn how to create a realistic travel plan, save money for your trip (and budget the right way), how to pack lighter and smarter, always stay safe, healthy, and happy on the road, meet people anywhere, keep your spirits up when plans inevitably go awry, and make the absolute most of your time exploring this beautiful planet.

Why should I take your advice?

I've traveled all over the world and lived to tell the tale. I've wandered all over Southeast Asia, backpacked the Gringo Trail from Mexico all the way to Argentina, explored every corner of Europe, traveled round-the-world (hopping between the continents), and much more. I'll tell you about the crazy highs and lows of adventure travel I've been through and share with you my most hard-won advice.

Is this book different from your blog?

Yes! Writing the book has been a totally separate project to my blog. While it may cover a few similar topics here and there, the book puts it all together in a way that only a proper book can.

Besides practical tips, I also go into the deeply personal aspects based on my years traveling around the world. I guarantee you that when you reach the final chapter, you'll feel fully inspired, prepared, and free of worries before you go off on your next trip.

What sort of travel does this cover?

The book is for anyone wanting to jump into the deep end of travel. That includes going on a backpacking or round-the-world trip, traveling as a digital nomad, but equally this guide is for you if you're simply going to a continent or country you've never been before.

The tips and advice can be used for trips of any length. But be warned: after reading this book, you may want to travel further or longer than you had planned!

Will the ebook work on my device?

The ebook version will work on any tablet, phone, ebook reader, or computer. You’ll get a Mobi version (for Kindle), ePub version (for any ebook reader), and a PDF version (can be read on any device).

START READING STRAIGHT AWAY

I thought I was going to Thailand for a 2-month trip. I was wrong. Things kind of escalated. I ended up travelling around the world for 2 years instead — and I’ve been a travel addict ever since.

I went to Thailand in part because I was frustrated with my life. I had just lost my job working in the video game industry in London. Many thought I had a dream job, but to be honest, it was fraught with endless meetings and corporate politics. It was the fourth job that slipped through my fingers due to downsizing or corporate strategic shifts.

I felt frustrated and needed a break from it all.

I arrived in Bangkok thinking I’d take this time to figure out my next steps… maybe emailing some recruiters and updating my CV while chilling on a beach somewhere.

I never even opened my CV. Instead, I got sucked into the world of travel. Thailand got me hooked, and then I simply couldn’t stop. I travelled all around Southeast Asia for nearly one year, and then from Mexico to Argentina in another. I burned through all my savings, but it was 100% worth it — in fact, it was the most epic, most fun, and the most fulfilling thing I’d ever done.

You see, to me travel isn’t just about relaxing or being on a holiday; it’s also about venturing out into the unknown, following your own path, and pushing beyond your comfort zone. It means trying new food, experiencing other cultures, and doing silly things sometimes. At its best, travel lets you view the world with a sense of awe and wonder.

It probably sounds cheesy, but travel let me rediscover who I am. And it helped me better understand the world I live in.

It’s also been a lot of stupid fun!

With this blog I hope to encourage others to travel more and to travel independently, by sharing real stories and honest advice.

I’m from The Netherlands (hoi!) and my mum is Polish (hence my first name). When I’m not exploring caves in Thailand or eating guinea pig in Peru, you can probably find me working from a park or cafe in beautiful sunny Lisbon, where I’m living most of the year these days.

Good luck on your travels, and thanks for stopping by!

- Katherine
